Dragonfly Spanish

AboutI've worked on learning a number of languages, but Spanish is the one I know best (after English). I feel that most programs focus too heavily on grammar and not enough on vocabulary, and some work too hard to be "conversational" to actually use the words for yourself (you end up just memorizing phrases). I wanted to build a site that teaches a foriegn language focusing on the most common words and putting them together grammatically so that you can say the things you want to say. This course was designed for self study so learners could be successful in a tradition Spanish 1 course.
What I LearnedSome of the challenges I faced with this project was the database design, the UX of the course, and creating some basic personalization so that the user is more invested in the project. Another of One of the takeaways from this was the difficulty in doing instructional design - setting things up to make it easy to learn is difficult. I liked figuring out how to delay the written words to show up in sync with the sound clips to make them more congruous for the user.
TechnologiesMongoDB, Express, Angular, Nodejs, Angular-Materialize, Angular-Audio, Angular-cookies

Silicon Valley Faces

AboutThis is a social media website for the non-profit Silicon Valley Faces, which teaches leadership skills to High Schoolers with the goal of building communities free of bias, bigotry and violence by transforming youth into community builders and empowering crime victims to thrive. The site was designed to help the organizers keep in touch with graduates/alumni and other stakeholders in the program.
What I LearnedThere were 8 of us, and learning to deal with merge conflicts, commenting code for others use, writing clearly, using good naming conventions, refactoring code and using includes to make the files more readable were all key. In addition, this project was developed according to the Scrum/Agile methodology, and I found the experience of breaking the project into tasks which we tracked with sticky notes on the Sprint Backlog to be valuable experience as well. Testing, deploying increments of the product, and then testing again to make sure it really works once it's out of production was important.
TechnologiesMongoDB, Express, Angular, Nodejs, Angular-Bootstrap, Regex, Youtube - Embed

Tiger Monkey Paw

AboutI'm always trying to teach myself new things, and I use youtube as one of my resources. A few years back I felt frustrated that I didn't have the tools to keep track of what videos I had watched, what I thought was good about each video, what timestamps I thought were particularly valuable, etc. This site was designed to rectify that problem. Users can keep track of what they like with the site, but it's optimized to help people learn.
What I LearnedThis was by far my most UX centered development experience. I went through several drafts as I condensed the site into a single page application and pushed through the technical challenges that I experienced as a result. I was especially happy with the way I manipulated the url so that users could pull directly from the browser window without requiring extra steps of the user. I also liked writing the regex expression used in the search function, and the algorithms that change the position value of every video in the list.
TechnologiesMongoDB, Express, Angular, Nodejs, Angular-Bootstrap, Regex

Domination

AboutThere are a lot of times in life where you want to work out, but don't own equipment and don't have time to make it to the gym. RentMe

AboutThe idea behind this app was that of the sharing economy - you can rent out that kayak in your garage when you're not using it and both you and the person you rent it to can benefit.
